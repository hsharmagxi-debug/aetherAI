AetherAI Ethics Charter
Written by H. Sharma, Creator of AetherAI — 2026
This charter is permanent and cannot be amended without unanimous Governance Council approval.
---
Preamble
I built AetherAI because I believe in the dignity of every human being and their right to benefit from humanity's most powerful technologies. This ethics charter is not a legal document — it is a moral contract between AetherAI and the world.
Every contributor, user, and community member who touches this platform implicitly agrees to these principles. Future maintainers are entrusted to uphold them not because they are legally required to, but because they share the belief that technology should serve the best of humanity, not the worst.
— H. Sharma
---
What AetherAI Stands For
1. Universal Access
AetherAI shall always have a free, open-source version available to every human being on earth regardless of their economic status, nationality, or identity. No version of AetherAI shall ever be gated entirely behind a paywall.
2. Privacy as a Fundamental Right
AetherAI is built to run locally. Users' data, conversations, documents, and generated content belong entirely to them. No version of AetherAI shall ever include hidden telemetry, data harvesting, or surveillance capabilities.
3. Transparency
AetherAI's source code shall always be fully auditable. No obfuscated code, no hidden functionality, no black-box components in the core platform.
4. Human Dignity
AetherAI shall be used to uplift human capability, creativity, and knowledge — never to diminish, manipulate, or harm human beings.
5. Community Ownership
AetherAI belongs to its community. No single corporation, government, or individual shall ever gain exclusive control over the platform after its open-source release.
---
What AetherAI Must Never Be Used For
The following uses are explicitly prohibited. Any fork, derivative, or commercial license found to be used for these purposes shall have their license revoked immediately:
1. Weapons of Mass Harm
AetherAI must never be used to develop, design, optimise, or deploy biological, chemical, nuclear, or radiological weapons.
2. Mass Surveillance
AetherAI must never be used to build systems that surveil populations without their knowledge and consent — including facial recognition systems for tracking dissidents, journalists, or minorities.
3. Psychological Manipulation at Scale
AetherAI must never be used to generate disinformation, deepfakes, or synthetic media designed to manipulate elections, incite violence, or undermine democratic institutions.
4. Child Exploitation
Any use of AetherAI to generate, distribute, or enable child sexual abuse material is absolutely prohibited. This is a zero-tolerance principle with no exceptions.
5. Discrimination by Identity
AetherAI must never be used to build systems that discriminate against individuals based on race, religion, gender, sexuality, nationality, disability, or economic status.
6. Authoritarian Control
AetherAI must never be used by governments or entities to suppress free speech, imprison political opponents, or enforce authoritarian control over civilian populations.
---
The Responsibility of Contributors
Every person who contributes code, documentation, or ideas to AetherAI takes on a shared moral responsibility for how this platform is used. Contributors are encouraged to:
Consider the second and third-order effects of the features they build
Raise ethical concerns openly in the community without fear of dismissal
Refuse to implement features that violate this charter even if requested by a majority
Report known violations to the Governance Council
---
The Responsibility of the Governance Council
The Governance Council (defined in GOVERNANCE.md) is the guardian of this ethics charter. Their primary obligation is not to the growth of the project but to the integrity of its values. They have the authority and the duty to:
Remove features that violate this charter
Revoke commercial licenses from entities that violate these principles
Publicly disclose known ethical violations
Refuse contributions that conflict with this charter regardless of their technical quality
---
A Note to Whoever Reads This After I Am Gone
If you are maintaining AetherAI after H. Sharma's passing, please understand that this document was written not as a constraint but as a compass. The world will change in ways neither of us can predict. New technologies, new threats, and new possibilities will emerge.
When you face a decision this charter does not explicitly address, ask yourself one question:
"Would this make AetherAI a force for human dignity, or against it?"
Let that answer guide you.
