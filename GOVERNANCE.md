AetherAI Governance Document
Established by H. Sharma, Creator and Founding Maintainer — 2026
---
Purpose
This document defines how AetherAI is governed — how decisions are made, who has authority, and most importantly, how the project continues and thrives after the passing of its creator.
AetherAI was built to outlast any single person. This governance structure exists to ensure that it does.
---
Governance Structure
Tier 1 — Founding Creator (H. Sharma)
During his lifetime, H. Sharma holds final authority over:
The project's strategic direction
Commercial license grants and revocations
Amendments to the Ethics Charter
Appointments to the Governance Council
This authority is non-transferable and expires upon his passing, at which point full authority transfers to the Governance Council.
---
Tier 2 — The Governance Council
The Governance Council is a body of 5 elected community members who collectively govern AetherAI. They are the permanent stewards of the project.
Responsibilities:
Maintaining the long-term vision of AetherAI
Approving major architectural changes
Granting and revoking commercial licenses
Resolving disputes within the community
Appointing Core Maintainers
Guarding the Ethics Charter
Council Composition:
5 members elected by the contributor community
Members serve 2-year terms with a maximum of 3 consecutive terms
Council decisions require a simple majority (3 of 5) for standard decisions
Ethics Charter amendments require unanimous approval (5 of 5)
Quorum requires 4 of 5 members present
First Council:
The first Governance Council shall be appointed by H. Sharma within 12 months of the project's first public release, or automatically constituted by the top 5 contributors by commit count at the time of his passing — whichever comes first.
---
Tier 3 — Core Maintainers
Core Maintainers are trusted developers with merge access to the main repository.
Responsibilities:
Reviewing and merging pull requests
Triaging issues and bugs
Mentoring new contributors
Maintaining documentation
Appointment:
Core Maintainers are appointed by the Governance Council based on sustained, high-quality contribution over a minimum of 6 months.

Tier 4 — Contributors
Anyone who submits a pull request, files an issue, improves documentation, or contributes to the community is a contributor. Contributors are the lifeblood of AetherAI.
See CONTRIBUTING.md for how to get started.

Decision Making Process
Standard Decisions (features, bug fixes, documentation)
Proposed via GitHub Pull Request or Issue
Reviewed by at least 2 Core Maintainers
Merged after approval with no outstanding objections for 72 hours
Major Decisions (architecture changes, new service integrations)
Proposed via GitHub Discussion with a minimum 14-day comment period
Requires approval of a majority of Core Maintainers
Governance Council has veto power
Governance Decisions (council membership, commercial licenses, ethics)
Proposed to the Governance Council directly
Minimum 30-day public comment period
Requires Council majority vote
Ethics Charter Amendments
Unanimous Governance Council approval required
Minimum 90-day public comment period
Cannot contradict the founding principles written by H. Sharma
---
Succession Protocol
Upon the Passing of H. Sharma
The following steps shall be taken within 30 days of confirmed notification:
Repository Access — The succession contact named in the private Succession Letter (held by a trusted individual) shall transfer repository ownership to the Governance Council.
Domain and Infrastructure — All domains, server credentials, and infrastructure access shall be transferred per the Succession Letter instructions.
Commercial Licensing Authority — Full commercial licensing authority transfers to the Governance Council. They may continue to grant commercial licenses under the same framework defined in COMMERCIAL_LICENSE.md.
Memorial — The Governance Council shall add a permanent memorial notice to the README.md acknowledging H. Sharma as the creator and his founding vision.
Continuity Statement — The Governance Council shall publish a public statement affirming their commitment to the Ethics Charter and the original mission of AetherAI.
If the Governance Council Dissolves
If the Governance Council cannot be constituted (fewer than 3 active members for more than 12 months), governance falls to the Apache Software Foundation or Linux Foundation under a pre-negotiated stewardship agreement, to be established by H. Sharma within 2 years of this document's creation.
---
Commercial License Governance
All commercial license revenue shall be allocated as follows:
40% — Project infrastructure and hosting costs
30% — Paid development bounties for critical features
20% — AetherAI Foundation reserve fund
10% — Community grants for contributors in lower-income regions
During H. Sharma's lifetime, he retains discretion over this allocation. After his passing, the Governance Council holds full authority.
---
Conflict Resolution
Disputes between contributors are resolved in this order:
Direct discussion between parties
Mediation by a Core Maintainer
Governance Council ruling (final and binding)
The Governance Council's ruling on ethics-related disputes is absolute and not subject to appeal.
---
Amendments
This governance document may be amended by the Governance Council with a majority vote and a 30-day public comment period. The founding principles in the Preamble of the Ethics Charter cannot be removed or weakened by any amendment.
